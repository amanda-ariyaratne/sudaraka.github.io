Welcome!

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Economics at Rice University. I obtained my Bachelor of Arts in Economics from New York University in 2021.

My research lies at the intersection of development, institutions, and economic history. I am interested in understanding how institutions, both past and present, shape the economic behaviours of individuals and influence economic development.

I have digitised and georeferenced a multitude of data on nineteenth-century Ceylon—including on plantations, agriculture, demographics, and the transport networks—which I have made publicly available [here].

Beyond economics, I am passionate about tea, antiques, and spin class. I also love reading Jane Austen and about the past and present of Sri Lanka.

Interests

My interest in literature focuses on 20th century southern American writers. I have a broad background in both American and European literature and am interested in both its cross-cultural and cross-regional influences. I also am interested in ways to improve student writing skills.

Research Interests

My research focuses on 20th century Southern Gothic literature, examining themes of poverty, alienation, crime, and violence in the works of major authors such as Flannery O'Connor, Carson McCullers, and William Faulkner.

Current Projects

  • Analyzing the representation of social isolation in Southern Gothic fiction
  • Cross-cultural influences in American and European literature
  • Pedagogical approaches to teaching Southern literature

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in encouraging students to actively engage with texts and share their interpretations. I promote collaborative learning and utilize online platforms to facilitate peer discussions and idea sharing.
